## Context

Spovio is a complete HR platform that ended up managing **1,200+ employees** across
10+ businesses. HR software lives or dies on getting the unglamorous rules right —
leave accrual, attendance, payroll — and on automating the manual back-and-forth
that drains an HR team. The goal was to model those rules correctly and **automate
80% of the workflow** around them.

## Architecture

A **React** frontend over a **Django** backend, with **PostgreSQL** for the system
of record. **Redis + Celery** drive time accrual and the scheduled/background work,
and **AWS S3** holds organization and employee documents securely.

## Modeling accrual & attendance

Time-off and attendance are built around **role-based accrual rates** — different
roles accrue leave on different rules. Getting this model right is what makes the
rest of the system trustworthy; accrual is computed and tracked through **Celery**
so balances stay correct over time without manual intervention.

## Payroll

The payroll module generates salaries **monthly** and exports to **Excel and PDF**,
turning what's usually a manual spreadsheet ritual into a repeatable, scheduled job.

<Callout type="tip" title="Background work is where HR automation lives">
  Accrual, monthly payroll runs and notifications are all scheduled, long-running
  tasks. Pushing them to Celery is what turns an HR tool from a data-entry form into
  something that actually runs the process for you.
</Callout>

## Notifications & automation

Email and SMS integrations drive HR alerts and approvals, closing the loop on
requests without someone chasing them. Together with scheduled accrual and payroll,
this is how the platform **automated roughly 80% of the HR workflow**.

## Outcome

Spovio turned manual HR processes into an automated system: correct role-based
accrual, scheduled monthly payroll with clean exports, secure document storage, and
alerting that moves approvals along — used across 10+ businesses managing 1,200+
employees.
