BoxedCMS started from a simple observation: publishing teams kept rebuilding the
same foundation — auth, theming, an editor, localization, ad monetization — and
every customization risked forking the core. The plug-and-play architecture
inverts that. Features register as plugins against a common contract, and themes
are uploaded as packages that the server resolves and re-renders on the fly, so
extending or restyling a site never means editing or redeploying the engine.

On top of that base, CKEditor provides rich-text editing and reusable content
templates, Passport.js unifies local, social and 2FA login behind one strategy
layer, dynamic translation services serve a single content source per locale,
and the Google AdSense APIs make monetization a first-class, configurable part
of the CMS rather than a bolt-on.
